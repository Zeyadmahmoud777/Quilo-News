EU Launches Ambitious AI Strategy to Reduce Dependence on U.S. and China

2025-10-05

Pursuing AI Sovereignty

In a bold move to assert technological autonomy, the European Union is introducing a new strategy dubbed “Apply AI,” aimed at cutting its dependence on foreign AI vendors, particularly from the U.S. and China. The plan, scheduled to be presented by EU tech chief Henna Virkkunen, emphasizes accelerating adoption of European-made generative AI systems in public administration, healthcare, manufacturing, and defense. With geopolitical tensions rising, Brussels sees tech independence not just as a matter of economics, but of security and resilience.

Strategies & Investments

To support the shift, the EU plans to mobilize approximately €1 billion through existing funds to back AI startups, foster research, and scale infrastructure. The proposal also highlights the importance of open-source models and modular systems that public agencies can adapt. By doing so, the EU intends not just to consume AI, but to become a major producer. Critics, however, warn that implementation will require navigating regulatory, talent, and procurement challenges.

Impacts & Challenges Ahead

This strategy could reshape the balance of power in AI development globally. European companies might find new opportunities, but also face stiff competition from established U.S. and Chinese firms. The EU will need to address data access, interoperability, security, and ethics to succeed. As AI becomes more entrenched in public services, the stakes for governance and control will only grow.
